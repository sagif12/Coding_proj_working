Regular blood pressure checks are crucial in identifying and managing hypertension, a major risk factor for heart disease and stroke. High blood pressure often has no symptoms, so monitoring your blood pressure is the only way to know if it's elevated and needs to be addressed.

Monitoring your heart rate is essential for assessing your cardiovascular health and detecting potential heart problems. A normal heart rate typically falls between 60 and 100 beats per minute, but your resting heart rate may vary depending on factors such as age, fitness level, and medication use. Regularly measuring your heart rate can provide valuable insights into your physical fitness and overall wellbeing, and can help you identify changes that may require medical attention.

Monitoring your blood glucose levels is essential for managing diabetes and preventing long-term complications such as nerve damage, kidney disease, and vision problems. Blood glucose levels can fluctuate throughout the day based on factors such as food intake, exercise, and medication use, making regular monitoring crucial for maintaining optimal glucose control.
